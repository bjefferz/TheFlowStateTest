Understanding Agriculture and Urban Runnoff

Agricultural and urban runoff are major sources of water pollution and originate from a wide range of
activities. Agricultural runoff occurs when water from rainfall or irrigation flows over farmland,
carrying with it pollutants such as synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, animal waste, and sediment into
nearby waterways. Urban runoff forms when rain or melting snow flows over streets, parking lots, and
other paved surfaces, collecting contaminants such as oil, grease, chemicals, metals, and trash.
Urban runoff can also overwhelm drainage systems, contributing to localized flooding. Both forms of
runoff can significantly degrade water quality, harming aquatic ecosystems and creating unsafe
conditions for human use. These impacts highlight the importance of effective runoff management to
protect drinking water sources, recreational areas, and environmental health.

What is runoff management?

Runoff management involves reducing, controlling, and treating stormwater to minimize pollution and
lower the risk of flooding. In the United States, the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) supports
these efforts through stormwater management programs that address runoff from both agricultural
and urban areas. Many cities and counties partner with the EPA to maintain storm drainage systems,
enforce local regulations, implement pollution-prevention strategies, and develop community
projects. The most accurate way to understand your county or city’s runoff management practices is
to visit their official website. These sites typically provide information on local regulations, project
maps, stormwater plans, and public outreach events or meetings. Local environmental agencies and
municipal departments often collaborate closely and are available to offer public education and
resources.

What can you do at home?

The community can also plays an important role in reducing runoff pollution, simple actions such as
properly disposing chemicals, painting vehicles to prevent any leakage. You can also keep pollutants
from washing into drains by installing a rain barrel, creating rain gardens and placing gutter guards,
and keeping up maintaince with your gutter. These small steps can significantly reduce the amount
of pollution entering our local waterways and contribute to a better environment.
